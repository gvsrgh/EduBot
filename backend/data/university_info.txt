[Fees & Payments]
- Tuition fees are paid via the University Student Portal. Accepted payment methods include credit/debit card and bank transfer.
- Payment Deadlines: Fall semester fees are due by September 15; Spring semester fees are due by February 1.
- Late Payments: A 5% late fee is applied after the deadline. Students who miss the deadline must coordinate with the Bursar's Office to complete payment.
- Payment Portal Access: Students can log in to the Student Portal at https://portal.university.edu using their student ID and password.
- Refund Policy: Refund requests must be submitted within 30 days of payment. A processing fee of $50 applies to all refunds.
- Payment Plans: Students experiencing financial difficulty can apply for a payment plan through the Bursar's Office. Payment plans allow splitting tuition into 3-4 monthly installments.

[Academic Programs]
- The Computer Science department offers a course on Database Systems where students learn SQL (Structured Query Language) for managing relational databases.
- The Database Systems course covers SQL fundamentals including SELECT queries, JOIN operations, aggregate functions, and transaction management.
- The university uses PostgreSQL as its primary database system for student records, course management, and administrative operations.
- Students can access SQL training resources through the library's online learning platform.
- Advanced courses include Distributed Databases, Big Data Analytics, and Cloud Computing with Database Integration.

[Admissions]
- Application Deadline: Applications for Fall semester admission must be submitted by March 1; Spring semester by October 1.
- Required Documents: Official transcripts, standardized test scores (SAT/ACT), two letters of recommendation, and personal statement.
- Application Fee: $75 (non-refundable). Fee waivers available for eligible applicants based on financial need.
- International Students: Must provide proof of English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS) and financial documentation.
- Transfer Students: Must have a minimum GPA of 2.5 from previous institution. Maximum 60 credits can be transferred.

[Student Services]
- Health Services: On-campus health center provides medical care, counseling, and wellness programs. Services are included in student fees.
- Library Services: Main library open 24/7 during academic year. Offers study rooms, computer labs, and research assistance.
- Career Center: Provides career counseling, resume reviews, mock interviews, and job placement assistance.
- Disability Services: Accommodations available for students with documented disabilities. Contact the Office of Accessibility Services.
- Housing: On-campus dormitories and apartments available. Housing application opens March 1 for Fall semester.

[Academic Policies]
- Attendance Policy: Students must attend at least 75% of classes to be eligible for final exams.
- Grading Scale: A (90-100), B (80-89), C (70-79), D (60-69), F (below 60).
- Academic Probation: Students with GPA below 2.0 are placed on academic probation and must meet with an advisor.
- Withdrawal Policy: Students can withdraw from courses until the 10th week of semester without academic penalty.
- Grade Appeals: Must be filed within 30 days of grade posting. Contact the Office of the Registrar.

[Campus Facilities]
- Main Library: Located in the center of campus, 5 floors with over 500,000 volumes.
- Recreation Center: Gym, swimming pool, basketball courts, and fitness classes. Open daily 6 AM - 11 PM.
- Dining Halls: Four dining halls across campus offering various meal plans. Meal plans required for students living on campus.
- Student Center: Houses student organizations, meeting rooms, game rooms, and food court.
- Parking: Student parking permits available for $200 per semester. Limited visitor parking in designated lots.

[Research Opportunities]
- Undergraduate Research: Students can participate in faculty-led research projects for academic credit.
- Summer Research Program: Paid research positions available during summer semester. Application deadline: February 15.
- Research Symposium: Annual event where students present research findings. Held every April.
- Grants: Research grants available for student projects up to $2,000. Apply through the Office of Research.

[Financial Aid]
- FAFSA Deadline: Submit Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) by April 15 for Fall semester.
- Scholarships: Merit-based and need-based scholarships available. Automatic consideration with admission application.
- Work-Study: Federal work-study positions available on campus. Apply through Financial Aid Office.
- Emergency Funds: Short-term emergency loans available for students facing unexpected financial hardship.
- Scholarship Database: Access searchable scholarship database at portal.university.edu/scholarships
